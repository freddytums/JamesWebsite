SiSolar

Sun Tracking Solar Panel

This project, SiSolar, was completed as my team's UTDallas Senior Design Project from 2019-2020.

Our mission was to develop a solar energy harnessing system which maintains maximum efficiency by tracking the sun throughout the day. Additional design requirements were to minimize the system's power consumption as well as avoiding the use of internet or external input.

The project is comprised of two assemblies: the Sun Angle Detector Assembly, and the Solar Panel Orienter Assembly.

The Sun Angle Detector Assembly is a light sensing circuit board that is illuminated with a pinhole projection of the sky. A micro-controller takes the circuit board's output, and triangulates the position of the sun.

The Solar Panel Orienter Assembly is a two axis motor that pitches and yaws the affixed solar panel.
